🛡️ Objective 1.3: Risk Management (The Next Breakdown)
1. Risk Identifiers
* Vulnerability: A weakness (e.g., an unpatched server).

* Threat: An outside force that can exploit a weakness (e.g., a hacker or a storm).

* Risk: The probability that a threat will exploit a vulnerability.

2. Risk Strategies (The "Big Four")
* Acceptance: Choosing to do nothing because the cost of fixing it is higher than the risk itself.

* Avoidance: Stopping the activity entirely (e.g., disabling a risky feature).

* Transference: Giving the risk to someone else (e.g., buying Cyber Insurance).

* Mitigation: Reducing the risk by adding controls (e.g., installing a firewall).

3. Quantitative Risk (The Math)
* SLE (Single Loss Expectancy): How much it costs if it happens once.

* ARO (Annual Rate of Occurrence): How many times it happens in a year.

* ALE (Annual Loss Expectancy): The total yearly cost (SLE x ARO = ALE).
